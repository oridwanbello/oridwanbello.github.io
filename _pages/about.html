I am a computational immunologist translating single-cell multi-omics and structural biology data into predictive and generative machine learning models of the immune system. My goal is to accelerate the discovery of broad-spectrum and targeted immunotherapies for humans and animals.

I earned my Ph.D. in Animal Sciences (Genetics and Genomics) from the University of Maryland, College Park. Prior to that, I completed my Bachelor's in Animal Science and a Master's in Animal Breeding and Genetics at the University of Ibadan, Nigeria, which provided me with a strong foundation in statistical genetics and quantitative biology.

My doctoral research, supervised by Dr. Philip Johnson and Dr. Li Ma, focused on identifying the drivers of shared/public T-cell receptors (TCRs). Public TCRs are identical receptor sequences shared across individuals at levels that challenge the classical paradigm of near-limitless TCR diversity. Applying deep learning and single-cell transcriptomics, we characterized the structural determinants of TCR publicness and identified distinct, heightened cytotoxic effector phenotypes in T cells expressing these shared receptors.
