I am passionate about enhancing human and animal health by unlocking the full potential of the immune system. I apply bioinformatics and machine learning to deconvolute the complexity of the immune system and accelerate translational solutions for human and animal health. I also explore selective breeding strategies to enhance animal health and productivity. I earned my Ph.D. from the Department of Animal and Avian Sciences, University of Maryland, College Park, USA. Prior to that, I earned my bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of Ibadan, Nigeria, in Animal Science and Animal Breeding & Genetics, respectively.

My doctoral research, under the supervision of Dr. Philip Johnson and Dr. Li Ma, explored the drivers of public T cell receptors using deep learning and single-cell transcriptomics. We provided insights into TCR publicness and identified distinct cellular phenotypes associated with T cells expressing public TCRs.
